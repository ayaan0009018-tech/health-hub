## Disease Image Place an image here: `images/hepatitis.jpg` ## Overview Hepatitis is a medical condition characterized by inflammation of the liver. The liver is one of the most important organs in the human body, responsible for filtering toxins, processing nutrients, producing bile for digestion, and helping regulate metabolism. When the liver becomes inflamed or damaged, its ability to perform these essential functions may be reduced. Hepatitis can be caused by viral infections, excessive alcohol consumption, certain medications, toxins, autoimmune diseases, and other medical conditions. The most common types of viral hepatitis are Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, Hepatitis C, Hepatitis D, and Hepatitis E. Each type has different modes of transmission, severity, and treatment options. Some people with hepatitis may experience mild symptoms or no symptoms at all, while others may develop serious liver damage. If left untreated, chronic hepatitis can lead to liver cirrhosis, liver failure, or liver cancer. Early diagnosis, proper treatment, vaccination (for certain types), and healthy lifestyle choices are important for preventing complications and protecting liver health. ## Causes * Hepatitis viruses (A, B, C, D, E) * Excessive alcohol consumption * Autoimmune diseases * Certain medications * Toxic chemical exposure * Fatty liver disease * Drug-related liver injury ## Risk Factors * Unprotected sexual contact * Sharing contaminated needles * Poor sanitation and hygiene * Excessive alcohol use * Blood exposure * Travel to high-risk areas * Weak immune system ## Prevention * Get recommended vaccinations * Practice good hygiene * Drink clean and safe water * Avoid sharing needles * Follow safe medical procedures * Limit alcohol consumption * Maintain a healthy lifestyle ## Health Advice * Follow medical treatment plans * Avoid alcohol completely if advised * Eat a balanced diet * Stay hydrated * Get regular liver function tests * Avoid unnecessary medications * Consult healthcare providers regularly ## Emergency Warning Signs Seek immediate medical attention if you experience: * Severe abdominal pain * Yellowing of the skin and eyes * Persistent vomiting * Confusion * Severe fatigue * Swelling of the abdomen * Bleeding problems ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) ### What is hepatitis? Hepatitis is inflammation of the liver caused by infections, toxins, autoimmune diseases, or other factors. ### Can hepatitis be prevented? Some types can be prevented through vaccination, good hygiene, and safe health practices. ### Is hepatitis contagious? Certain viral forms, such as Hepatitis A, B, and C, can spread from one person to another through different routes. ### Can hepatitis lead to liver cancer? Chronic Hepatitis B and C infections may increase the risk of liver cancer if not properly managed. ## Related Diseases * Fatty Liver Disease * Liver Cirrhosis * Liver Cancer * Alcohol-Related Liver Disease * Gallbladder Disorders ## Disclaimer This information is for educational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
