# Obesity ## Disease Image Place an image here: `images/obesity.jpg` ## Overview Obesity is a medical condition in which excessive body fat accumulates to a level that may negatively affect health. It is one of the most common health problems worldwide and can increase the risk of many serious diseases, including diabetes, heart disease, high blood pressure, stroke, sleep apnea, and certain types of cancer. Obesity is often measured using the Body Mass Index (BMI), which compares a person's weight to their height. A BMI of 30 or higher is generally considered obese. However, BMI is only a screening tool and may not accurately reflect body fat in all individuals. The condition develops when a person consistently consumes more calories than they burn through daily activities and exercise. Modern lifestyles, unhealthy eating habits, lack of physical activity, stress, insufficient sleep, and genetic factors can all contribute to obesity. Obesity can affect physical health, mental well-being, self-confidence, and quality of life. Fortunately, many cases can be prevented or managed through healthy lifestyle changes, regular exercise, proper nutrition, and medical guidance when necessary. ## Causes * Overeating and unhealthy diet * Lack of physical activity * Genetic factors * Hormonal disorders * Stress and emotional eating * Poor sleep habits * Certain medications * Sedentary lifestyle ## Risk Factors * Family history of obesity * High-calorie diet * Physical inactivity * Lack of sleep * Stress * Diabetes * Hormonal imbalances * Increasing age ## Prevention * Eat balanced and nutritious meals * Exercise regularly * Limit sugary drinks and junk food * Get enough sleep * Manage stress effectively * Monitor weight regularly * Maintain an active lifestyle ## Health Advice * Follow a healthy eating plan * Drink plenty of water * Engage in daily physical activity * Reduce processed food intake * Consult healthcare professionals when needed * Set realistic weight-loss goals * Track your progress regularly ## Emergency Warning Signs Seek medical attention if obesity is associated with: * Severe breathing difficulties * Chest pain * Sleep-related breathing problems * Sudden swelling * High blood pressure complications * Signs of heart disease ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) ### What is obesity? Obesity is a condition involving excessive body fat that may harm health. ### Can obesity cause other diseases? Yes. It increases the risk of diabetes, heart disease, hypertension, and many other conditions. ### Is exercise important? Yes. Regular exercise helps maintain a healthy weight and improves overall health. ### Can obesity be treated? Yes.
