# Diabetes ## Disease Image Place an image here: `images/diabetes.jpg` ## Overview Diabetes is a chronic disease that affects how the body uses glucose (blood sugar). Glucose is the primary source of energy for the body's cells. In people with diabetes, the body either does not produce enough insulin or cannot effectively use the insulin it produces. As a result, blood sugar levels become too high and may lead to serious health complications if not properly managed. ## Causes * Insufficient insulin production * Insulin resistance * Genetic factors * Obesity * Unhealthy lifestyle ## Risk Factors * Family history of diabetes * Overweight or obesity * Lack of physical activity * High blood pressure * Poor diet * Increasing age ## Prevention * Maintain a healthy body weight * Exercise regularly * Eat a balanced diet rich in vegetables and whole grains * Limit sugary foods and drinks * Get regular health checkups ## Health Advice * Monitor blood sugar levels regularly * Take medicines as prescribed * Stay physically active * Drink enough water * Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption * Follow your doctor's recommendations ## Emergency Warning Signs Seek immediate medical help if you experience: * Severe weakness * Confusion * Difficulty breathing * Loss of consciousness * Extremely high blood sugar levels ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) ### Can diabetes be cured? Currently, diabetes cannot always be cured, but it can be effectively managed. ### Can exercise help? Yes. Regular physical activity helps control blood sugar levels. ### Is sugar completely forbidden? Not necessarily. It should be consumed in moderation according to medical advice. ### Why are regular checkups important? Regular checkups help prevent complications and monitor treatment effectiveness. ## Related Diseases * Obesity * Heart Disease * Hypertension * Kidney Disease ## Disclaimer This information is for educational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice.

Overview

Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how the body uses blood sugar (glucose). It can lead to serious health problems if not managed properly.

Symptoms

  • Frequent urination
  • Excessive thirst
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision

Causes

Diabetes occurs when the body does not produce enough insulin or cannot use insulin effectively.

Prevention

  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Exercise regularly
  • Eat balanced meals
  • Avoid excessive sugar intake

Health Advice

  • Monitor blood sugar levels
  • Take prescribed medication
  • Stay physically active
  • Attend regular medical checkups

When to See a Doctor

Consult a healthcare professional if symptoms worsen or blood sugar levels become difficult to control.
