Tuberculosis (TB)

Overview

Tuberculosis is a bacterial infection that mainly affects the lungs.

Symptoms

  • Persistent cough
  • Weight loss
  • Night sweats
  • Fever

Causes

  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis bacteria

Prevention

  • Early diagnosis
  • Good ventilation
  • Vaccination where recommended

Health Advice

  • Take medicines regularly
  • Complete full treatment
  • Attend follow-up appointments

# Tuberculosis (TB) ## Disease Image Place an image here: `images/tuberculosis.jpg` ## Overview Tuberculosis (TB) is a serious infectious disease caused by the bacterium *Mycobacterium tuberculosis*. It most commonly affects the lungs, but it can also affect other parts of the body such as the brain, kidneys, spine, and lymph nodes. TB spreads through the air when a person with active tuberculosis in the lungs coughs, sneezes, laughs, speaks, or sings. People nearby may inhale the bacteria and become infected. However, not everyone infected with TB bacteria becomes sick. Some individuals develop latent TB infection, where the bacteria remain inactive in the body and cause no symptoms. Latent TB can later become active if the immune system weakens. Tuberculosis remains a major health problem in many countries. Early diagnosis, proper treatment, and completing the full course of medication are essential for preventing complications and stopping the spread of the disease. Modern treatments are highly effective when taken correctly. With proper medical care, most people with TB can recover completely. ## Causes * Infection by *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* * Close contact with a person who has active TB * Exposure to infected airborne droplets * Weak immune system increasing susceptibility ## Risk Factors * Living with someone who has active TB * HIV infection * Diabetes * Malnutrition * Smoking * Alcohol abuse * Weak immune system * Overcrowded living conditions ## Prevention * Early detection and treatment * Good ventilation indoors * Covering mouth when coughing or sneezing * Avoiding close contact with active TB patients when advised * Following public health recommendations * Maintaining a healthy immune system * TB vaccination where recommended ## Health Advice * Take all prescribed medications exactly as directed * Complete the full treatment course * Attend regular follow-up appointments * Eat nutritious foods * Get adequate rest * Avoid smoking and alcohol * Inform healthcare providers about worsening symptoms ## Emergency Warning Signs Seek immediate medical attention if you experience: * Coughing blood * Severe difficulty breathing * Chest pain that worsens * High persistent fever * Severe weakness * Confusion or altered mental status ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) ### Is tuberculosis contagious? Active pulmonary TB can spread through the air from one person to another. ### Can TB be cured? Yes. Most cases can be cured with proper treatment and completion of medication. ### How long does treatment take? Treatment often lasts several months, depending on the patient's condition and medical advice. ### Why is completing treatment important? Stopping treatment early can lead to drug-resistant TB and treatment failure. ## Related Diseases * Pneumonia * COVID-19 * Bronchitis * Asthma * Chronic Lung Diseases ## Disclaimer This information is for educational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
