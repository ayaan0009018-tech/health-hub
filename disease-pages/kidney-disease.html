# Kidney Disease ## Disease Image Place an image here: `images/kidney-disease.jpg` ## Overview Kidney disease refers to conditions that damage the kidneys and reduce their ability to perform important functions. The kidneys are two bean-shaped organs located on either side of the spine. They help filter waste products, excess fluids, and toxins from the blood, regulate blood pressure, balance electrolytes, and support the production of red blood cells. When the kidneys become damaged, waste materials can build up in the body and lead to serious health complications. Kidney disease may develop gradually over many years, known as chronic kidney disease (CKD), or occur suddenly due to injury, infection, or other medical conditions. Many people with early kidney disease may not notice symptoms. As the condition progresses, symptoms such as swelling, fatigue, and changes in urination may appear. Early diagnosis and treatment are important to slow disease progression and protect kidney function. Common causes include diabetes, high blood pressure, infections, and inherited disorders. Maintaining a healthy lifestyle and managing underlying conditions can significantly reduce the risk of kidney disease. ## Causes * Diabetes * High blood pressure * Kidney infections * Autoimmune diseases * Genetic kidney disorders * Kidney stones * Long-term use of certain medications * Severe dehydration ## Risk Factors * Diabetes * Hypertension * Family history of kidney disease * Obesity * Smoking * Older age * Heart disease * Poor lifestyle habits ## Prevention * Control blood sugar levels * Maintain healthy blood pressure * Drink adequate water * Eat a balanced diet * Exercise regularly * Avoid smoking * Limit excessive use of medications unless prescribed * Get regular health checkups ## Health Advice * Follow medical treatment plans * Monitor kidney function regularly * Reduce salt intake * Maintain a healthy weight * Stay physically active * Avoid self-medication * Consult a doctor before taking supplements ## Emergency Warning Signs Seek immediate medical attention if you experience: * Severe swelling of legs or face * Difficulty breathing * Very little or no urination * Severe fatigue * Persistent nausea and vomiting * Chest pain * Confusion ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) ### What do the kidneys do? The kidneys filter waste products, balance fluids, and help regulate blood pressure. ### Can kidney disease be cured? Some kidney conditions can be treated successfully, while chronic kidney disease often requires long-term management. ### Is diabetes linked to kidney disease? Yes. Diabetes is one of the leading causes of kidney disease worldwide. ### How can kidney disease be prevented? Maintaining healthy blood pressure, controlling diabetes, staying active, and regular checkups can help prevent kidney disease. ## Related Diseases * Diabetes * Hypertension * Heart Disease * Kidney Stones * Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) ## Disclaimer This information is for educational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
