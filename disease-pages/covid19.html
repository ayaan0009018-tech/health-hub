COVID-19

Overview

COVID-19 is a respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus.

Symptoms

  • Fever
  • Cough
  • Fatigue
  • Loss of taste or smell

Causes

  • Coronavirus infection

Prevention

  • Wash hands frequently
  • Stay home when sick
  • Maintain hygiene

Health Advice

  • Rest and hydrate
  • Monitor symptoms
  • Consult a doctor if breathing becomes difficult

# COVID-19 ## Disease Image Place an image here: `images/covid.jpg` ## Overview COVID-19 is an infectious respiratory disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It was first identified in late 2019 and quickly spread worldwide, leading to a global pandemic. COVID-19 primarily affects the respiratory system, but it can also impact the heart, brain, kidneys, and other organs. The disease spreads mainly through respiratory droplets and small airborne particles released when an infected person coughs, sneezes, talks, or breathes. Symptoms can range from very mild to severe. Some individuals may have no symptoms at all, while others may require hospitalization. Most people recover within a few weeks, but some experience long-term symptoms known as "Long COVID." These symptoms may include fatigue, breathing difficulties, and problems with concentration. Older adults, people with chronic diseases, and those with weakened immune systems are at greater risk of severe illness. Vaccination, good hygiene practices, and staying home when sick remain important ways to reduce the spread of COVID-19. ## Causes * Infection with the SARS-CoV-2 virus * Close contact with infected individuals * Exposure to respiratory droplets or airborne particles * Contact with contaminated surfaces followed by touching the face (less common) ## Risk Factors * Older age * Heart disease * Diabetes * Obesity * Chronic lung disease * Weak immune system * Living in crowded environments ## Prevention * Wash hands regularly * Cover coughs and sneezes * Stay home when sick * Maintain good ventilation indoors * Follow public health recommendations * Stay up to date with recommended vaccinations * Avoid close contact with infected individuals ## Health Advice * Rest adequately * Drink plenty of fluids * Monitor symptoms carefully * Follow medical advice * Eat nutritious foods * Seek testing when appropriate * Protect vulnerable family members ## Emergency Warning Signs Seek immediate medical attention if you experience: * Difficulty breathing * Persistent chest pain * Confusion * Inability to stay awake * Blue lips or face * Severe weakness ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) ### What causes COVID-19? COVID-19 is caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. ### Can COVID-19 be prevented? Risk can be reduced through hygiene, ventilation, vaccination, and avoiding exposure to infected individuals. ### Can COVID-19 become severe? Yes. Some individuals may develop severe complications requiring hospital care. ### What is Long COVID? Long COVID refers to symptoms that continue for weeks or months after the initial infection. ## Related Diseases * Pneumonia * Influenza * Asthma * Bronchitis * Tuberculosis ## Disclaimer This information is for educational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
