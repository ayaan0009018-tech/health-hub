Dengue Fever

Overview

Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral infection common in tropical regions.

Symptoms

  • High fever
  • Severe headache
  • Muscle and joint pain
  • Skin rash

Causes

  • Aedes mosquito bites

Prevention

  • Use mosquito nets
  • Remove standing water
  • Apply mosquito repellent

Health Advice

  • Drink plenty of fluids
  • Rest adequately
  • Seek medical care if symptoms worsen

Dengue Fever

# Dengue Fever ## Disease Image Place an image here: `images/dengue.jpg` ## Overview Dengue fever is a viral infection spread by the bite of infected Aedes mosquitoes, especially Aedes aegypti. It is common in tropical and subtropical regions and often increases during the rainy season when mosquito breeding sites become more common. Dengue can affect people of all ages. Some people may experience mild symptoms, while others can develop severe dengue, which is a medical emergency. Early diagnosis and proper medical care are important to prevent complications. Symptoms usually appear 4–10 days after a mosquito bite and may last for several days. Most people recover completely with supportive care, rest, and adequate hydration. However, severe cases may involve bleeding, organ damage, or dangerously low blood pressure. Since there is no specific cure for dengue in most cases, prevention mainly focuses on avoiding mosquito bites and reducing mosquito breeding areas. ## Causes * Infection with the dengue virus * Bite of an infected Aedes mosquito * Living in areas with high mosquito populations * Poor mosquito control measures ## Risk Factors * Living in tropical or subtropical regions * Previous dengue infection * Exposure to mosquito breeding areas * Lack of mosquito protection * Traveling to dengue-prone regions ## Prevention * Use mosquito repellents * Sleep under mosquito nets when necessary * Wear full-sleeved clothing * Remove standing water around homes * Keep water containers covered * Install window screens * Support community mosquito-control programs ## Health Advice * Drink plenty of fluids * Get adequate rest * Monitor fever and symptoms * Follow medical advice * Seek medical attention if symptoms worsen * Avoid self-medication without professional guidance ## Emergency Warning Signs Seek immediate medical care if you experience: * Severe abdominal pain * Persistent vomiting * Bleeding from gums or nose * Blood in vomit or stool * Extreme weakness * Difficulty breathing * Sudden drop in blood pressure ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) ### Is dengue contagious? No. Dengue does not usually spread directly from person to person. It spreads through infected mosquito bites. ### Can dengue occur more than once? Yes. A person can be infected by different dengue virus types more than once. ### Is dengue dangerous? Most cases are mild, but severe dengue can be life-threatening and requires immediate treatment. ### How can dengue be prevented? Prevent mosquito bites and eliminate mosquito breeding sites. ## Related Diseases * Malaria * Chikungunya * Zika Virus Infection * Viral Fever * Yellow Fever ## Disclaimer This information is for educational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
