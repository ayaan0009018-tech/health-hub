# Migraine ## Disease Image Place an image here: `images/migraine.jpg` ## Overview Migraine is a neurological disorder that causes recurrent headaches, often accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound. Unlike a normal headache, a migraine can be severe and may last from a few hours to several days. It can interfere with daily activities, work, school, and social life. Migraines affect millions of people worldwide and are more common in women than men. The exact cause of migraine is not fully understood, but experts believe it involves changes in brain activity, nerve signals, blood vessels, and certain chemicals in the brain. Some people experience warning signs called an "aura" before a migraine attack. These may include visual disturbances such as flashing lights, blind spots, or tingling sensations in different parts of the body. However, not everyone experiences an aura. Migraine attacks may be triggered by stress, lack of sleep, dehydration, hormonal changes, certain foods, strong smells, or environmental factors. Identifying and avoiding personal triggers can help reduce the frequency of attacks.
