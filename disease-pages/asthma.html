# Asthma ## Disease Image Place an image here: `images/asthma.jpg` ## Overview Asthma is a long-term respiratory disease that affects the airways in the lungs. In people with asthma, the airways become inflamed, swollen, and sensitive to certain triggers. This can make breathing difficult and cause episodes known as asthma attacks. Asthma can affect people of all ages, but it often begins during childhood. Although asthma cannot always be cured, it can usually be managed effectively with proper treatment and lifestyle changes. People with asthma can live healthy and active lives if they follow their treatment plans and avoid known triggers. Common triggers include dust, pollen, smoke, cold air, respiratory infections, strong odors, and physical exercise. The severity of asthma varies from person to person. Some individuals experience mild symptoms occasionally, while others may have frequent and severe attacks. Understanding asthma and taking preventive measures can significantly reduce symptoms and improve quality of life. ## Causes * Genetic factors * Family history of asthma * Allergies * Respiratory infections during childhood * Environmental pollution * Exposure to tobacco smoke * Occupational exposure to chemicals and dust ## Risk Factors * Family history of asthma * Allergic conditions such as eczema * Exposure to smoke * Obesity * Air pollution * Frequent respiratory infections * Living in environments with allergens ## Prevention * Avoid smoking and secondhand smoke * Keep living areas clean * Reduce exposure to dust and allergens * Use air filters if needed * Follow prescribed medications * Get recommended vaccinations * Avoid known asthma triggers ## Health Advice * Always carry your prescribed inhaler * Follow your doctor's treatment plan * Exercise regularly with medical guidance * Maintain a healthy diet * Stay hydrated * Monitor symptoms and triggers * Attend regular medical checkups ## Emergency Warning Signs Seek immediate medical attention if: * Severe shortness of breath * Difficulty speaking due to breathlessness * Blue lips or fingernails * Rapid worsening of symptoms * Inhaler not providing relief * Chest tightness becomes severe ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) ### Can asthma be cured? Asthma may not always be cured, but it can usually be controlled effectively. ### Is exercise safe for asthma patients? Yes. Many people with asthma can exercise safely when following medical advice. ### What is an asthma attack? An asthma attack occurs when airways become severely narrowed, making breathing difficult. ### Should inhalers be used regularly? Use inhalers exactly as prescribed by your healthcare provider. ## Related Diseases * Pneumonia * COVID-19 * Bronchitis * Allergic Rhinitis * Chronic Respiratory Diseases ## Disclaimer This information is for educational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
