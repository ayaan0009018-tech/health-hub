Malaria

Overview

Malaria is a serious disease caused by parasites transmitted through mosquito bites.

Symptoms

  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Sweating
  • Headache

Causes

  • Parasites transmitted by infected mosquitoes

Prevention

  • Sleep under mosquito nets
  • Use insect repellents
  • Avoid mosquito breeding areas

Health Advice

  • Get tested early
  • Complete prescribed medication
  • Stay hydrated

# Malaria ## Disease Image Place an image here: `images/malaria.jpg` ## Overview Malaria is a serious infectious disease caused by parasites of the *Plasmodium* family. It spreads to humans through the bite of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes. Malaria remains a major public health concern in many tropical and subtropical countries, especially in regions with warm climates and poor mosquito control. The disease can affect people of all ages. Symptoms usually appear 10 to 15 days after being bitten by an infected mosquito, although the timing may vary depending on the type of parasite involved. If diagnosed early, malaria can usually be treated successfully. However, if left untreated, it can become severe and may lead to life-threatening complications. Malaria affects red blood cells and can cause recurring episodes of fever, chills, and sweating. Severe malaria can damage vital organs such as the brain, kidneys, and lungs. Children, pregnant women, older adults, and people with weakened immune systems are at higher risk of complications. Preventing mosquito bites and seeking prompt medical care are essential for reducing the impact of malaria. ## Causes * Infection by *Plasmodium* parasites * Bite of an infected female Anopheles mosquito * Rarely through infected blood transfusions or contaminated needles * Transmission from mother to baby during pregnancy in some cases ## Risk Factors * Living in malaria-endemic areas * Traveling to regions where malaria is common * Lack of mosquito protection * Poor sanitation and stagnant water * Pregnancy * Weak immune system * Young children ## Prevention * Sleep under insecticide-treated mosquito nets * Use mosquito repellents * Wear long-sleeved clothing * Install window and door screens * Eliminate standing water around homes * Follow travel-related malaria prevention advice * Seek early testing if symptoms develop ## Health Advice * Seek medical attention if fever develops after mosquito exposure * Complete the full course of prescribed medication * Stay hydrated * Get adequate rest * Monitor symptoms carefully * Avoid self-medicating without professional guidance ## Emergency Warning Signs Seek immediate medical care if you experience: * Difficulty breathing * Seizures * Confusion or unconsciousness * Severe weakness * Persistent vomiting * Signs of severe dehydration * Yellowing of the eyes or skin ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) ### Is malaria contagious? Malaria does not usually spread directly from person to person. It is mainly transmitted through infected mosquito bites. ### Can malaria be cured? Yes. Many forms of malaria can be treated successfully with appropriate medications when diagnosed early. ### Who is most at risk? Young children, pregnant women, travelers, and people with weakened immune systems are at greater risk. ### How can I reduce my risk? Use mosquito protection measures and seek medical care promptly if symptoms appear. ## Related Diseases * Dengue * Chikungunya * Viral Fever * Yellow Fever * Zika Virus Infection ## Disclaimer This information is for educational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
